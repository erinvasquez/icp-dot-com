Oscillator Project

A sin-wave oscillator program that outputs audio signals via the Unity 3D Engine's audio system. With this simple sin-wave oscillator, we can control a variety of factors to control the sound we create. Several different waveform types can be used, and can be added or multiplied onto each other, creating unique sound types. At the moment, keyboard input is being rigged via Unity 2020's newest Input System. In the future, an entire synthesizer comprised of QWERTY keyboard controls. As an extra effect, I intend to have the on-screen keyboard, as well as the surrounding 3D environment, react to the user's input. Either via the key they play, or the timing used in between keystrokes. BPM Detection may be needed to accomplish this.

VR Oscillator Project

Building on the previously made oscillator, I found that the oscillator's pitch and volume could be manipulated using some simple controls. Height and distance values fed from a pair of Steam VR controllers are an incredible way to interact with music using the player's body. Similar to how a trombone player's stretched arm length determines the pitch of the note played on the instrument, the distance from the ground determines either pitch or volume. Improvements or even specializations in this design will be considered in creating future VR instruments.
